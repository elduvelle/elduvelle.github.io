1.Home bases.
A home base is a place that animals repeatedly return to & pause at. They might be anchoring the map of space and/or recalibrating path integration.
In 3D, they tend to be at the bottom layer & to be stable across days:

2. Excursions.
In 2D, rodents do excursions from their home bases, with exploratory outbound trips and direct return trips.
Similar in 3D: long outbound trips with more xy moves, direct returns with rats usually going down to home base in one go, disregarding food on the way:

3. More on homebases: they were present in the corners of a low-walled arena but spreadout in a high-walled arena. This might be to maximize visual spatial information gathering.
(safety is also a known parameter influencing home base location, apparently not a lot in our case)

4. Tilted 'hypercube': cube axes allowed equal movement in x,y & z. Home bases were more spreadout & less stable BUT rats did more horizontal movements than vertical ones, as in the flat hypercube.
Perhaps rats were a bit lost but still minimised their energy expenditure?
